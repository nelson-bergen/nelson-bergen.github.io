Reliability Test Fixture

R&D Co-op • Fresenius NxStage

The Challenge

We needed a way to reliably cycle-test a peristaltic pump used within one of our dialysate preparation units, to determine its failure methods and lifecycle. The challenge was to create a comprehensive testing solution that could simulate extended use while monitoring performance metrics.

My Solution

I created a test fixture that included mounts for 5 pumps on a wire rack, which on a timer would run fluid through the pumps over a set period of time. Using equipment already available in the lab and shop spaces, I designed custom mounting blocks and fastening hardware using SolidWorks, before fabricating them out of aluminum stock using a 2-axis CNC Mill, belt sander, and band saw.

One key challenge was that the DC motors generated significant heat over time. To address this, I used wire racks to encourage natural convective air cooling and designed mounting blocks to raise motors away from heat-sensitive components like STEP drivers and power sources.

Beyond the fixture, I developed comprehensive lifecycle testing procedures and failure criteria for volume flow rate and sound level based on system specifications and worst-case environmental conditions.

Impact & Results

Accelerated Testing: Simulated 1 year of use over 3 months, allowing rapid validation of new pump designs

The fixture enabled longer testing periods without motor cooling interruptions and was designed to be expandable for future testing needs. Through my lifecycle testing procedures, we identified failure methods that would have otherwise gone unnoticed.

Pressure Hammering Investigation

R&D Co-op • Fresenius NxStage

The Challenge

Manufacturing identified that a sensor in one of our products was experiencing reliability issues, resulting in abnormally high pressure readings that triggered safety alarms. A theory proposed by our team was that pressure hammering from rapid valve closure may have been causing mechanical deformation of the sensor body. I was tasked with leading an investigation to determine whether pressure hammering was occurring in our system.

My Approach

I developed a series of tests to measure the presence of pressure hammering and identify variables impacting system pressure variation, including fluid pathway configuration, user interactions, and emergency valve closures.

To overcome the limited resolution of the system's built-in measurement utility, I connected the sensor directly to an oscilloscope to catch rapid pressure spikes. With help from senior technicians and electrical engineers, I learned to amplify and clean the signal using fixtures and capacitors.

I wrote a Python script that communicated with the oscilloscope using SCPI standards to automate data collection, automatically recording maximum voltage and frequency upon voltage trigger events.

Results & Impact

Using this test fixture, I successfully documented the pressure waveform within the system's fluid pathway and provided evidence of potential pressure hammering. Although another phenomenon was ultimately determined to be responsible for the sensor reliability issue, my investigation prompted further research into pressure hammering effects within the system.
